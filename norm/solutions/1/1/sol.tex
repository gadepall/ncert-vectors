The direction vector of the line joining \myvec{4\\7\\8}, \myvec{2\\3\\4} 
is 
\begin{align}
	\vec{m}_1 &= \myvec{4\\7\\8} -  \myvec{2\\3\\4} 
	\\
		  &= \myvec{2\\4\\4}
\end{align}
Similarly, 
the direction vector of the line joining \myvec{-1\\-2\\1}, \myvec{1\\2\\5} is 
\begin{align}
	\vec{m}_2 &= \myvec{-1\\-2\\1}- \myvec{1\\2\\5}
	\\
		  &= \myvec{-2\\-4\\-4} = - \vec{m}_1
		  \label{eq:basic/1/6}
\end{align}
By definition, from
		  \eqref{eq:basic/1/6},
the lines with direction vectors $\vec{m}_1, \vec{m}_2$  are  parallel.
