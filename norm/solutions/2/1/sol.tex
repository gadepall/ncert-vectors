Let 
\begin{align}
    \vec{C} &= \vec{A} + \vec{B} \\
\end{align}

\begin{align}
    \therefore \vec{C} &= \myvec{4\\3\\-2}\\
\text{and }    \norm{\vec{C}} &= \sqrt{(4)^2 + (3)^2 + (-2)^2}\\
&= \sqrt{29}
\end{align}
Thus, 
the unit vector in the direction of $\vec{C}$ is 
\begin{align}
    \frac{\vec{C}}{\norm{\vec{C}}}
 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{29}} \myvec{4\\3\\-2}
\end{align}
